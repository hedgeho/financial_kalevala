## üéØ Educational Goals

This game teaches teenagers essential financial concepts through character-driven narrative:

1. **FOMO & Impulse Control** - Resisting pressure to make quick decisions
2. **Due Diligence** - Questioning too-good-to-be-true offers and seeking verification
3. **Social Pressure Resistance** - Making decisions based on personal values, not peer pressure
4. **Opportunity Cost** - Understanding what you give up when you spend money
5. **Critical Thinking** - Recognizing manipulation tactics (urgency, scarcity, social proof)
6. **Multiple Perspectives** - Understanding that different financial philosophies have value

## üí° How the Companion System Works

### Active Advisors Throughout
Your three companions actively debate with each other and react to your decisions in real-time:
- They chime in during merchant pitches
- They argue about the best course of action
- They react emotionally to your choices
- They reflect on outcomes from their unique perspectives

### Hidden Approval System
Each companion tracks their opinion of your choices, but this is never shown numerically:
- Choose impulsively ‚Üí Lemmink√§inen approves, Aino worries
- Choose investigation ‚Üí Ilmarinen approves, Lemmink√§inen frustrated
- Choose caution ‚Üí Aino proud, everyone respects restraint

Approval affects tone, dialogue, and willingness to help‚Äîbut you learn by observing their reactions, not by watching meters.

### Multi-Perspective Reflections
After each scenario, all three companions share their wisdom:
- Each views the same event through their financial philosophy
- Lemmink√§inen learns about the trap of excitement
- Aino emphasizes security and planning
- Ilmarinen explains manipulation tactics
- Together they show the full picture of financial wisdom

## üéÆ Design Philosophy

### Companion Personalities as Teaching Tools
Each companion embodies a real financial mindset:
- **Aino**: People who prioritize security and fear loss
- **Ilmarinen**: People who research and verify before committing
- **Lemmink√§inen**: People drawn to excitement and "opportunities"

Teenagers see themselves in these characters and learn that all perspectives have value‚Äîbut some serve you better in specific situations.

### Learning Through Character
Instead of lecturing, the game teaches through:
- Observing companions react to pressure
- Seeing excitement turn to regret
- Watching skepticism prove correct
- Experiencing the consequences of choices

### Respect for Player Agency
- No judgment for "wrong" choices
- Every path includes valuable lessons
- Companions offer advice but never force decisions
- Hidden approval means authentic reactions, not score-chasing
